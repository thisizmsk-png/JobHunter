# Iron Temple -- Complete 12-Week Training Program

> 48 sessions | Upper/Lower 4-day split | 3 mesocycles | Progressive overload via double progression
> Intermediate lifter | 75 min max per session | Lean aesthetic focus

---

## How to Read This Program

### Notation Key

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **SS** | Superset -- perform the two exercises back-to-back with no rest between them, then rest the indicated time before the next round |
| **RIR** | Reps In Reserve -- how many more reps you *could* have done. RIR 2 means you stopped 2 reps short of failure |
| **Myo-rep** | A rest-pause technique: perform an activation set of 12-15 reps to near failure, rest 10-15 seconds, then do 3-5 mini-sets of 3-5 reps with the same weight, resting 10-15 seconds between each mini-set. Counts as 1 "set" in the volume tally but delivers ~3-4 effective sets of stimulus |
| **3x8-12** | 3 sets of 8 to 12 reps (double progression range) |
| **3x10/leg** | 3 sets of 10 reps per leg |
| **+5 lbs** | Increase load by 5 pounds |
| **MEV** | Minimum Effective Volume -- the lowest set count that produces measurable growth |
| **MAV** | Maximum Adaptive Volume -- the volume that produces the best gains |
| **MRV** | Maximum Recoverable Volume -- the most you can do and still recover from |

### Double Progression Method

1. You are given a rep range (e.g., 8-12).
2. Start at the bottom of the range with a weight that puts you at the prescribed RIR.
3. Each session, try to add reps while keeping RIR targets.
4. When you hit the TOP of the range for ALL prescribed sets, increase the load:
   - Compounds (squat, bench, row, RDL, OHP): +5-10 lbs
   - Isolations (curls, laterals, extensions): +2.5-5 lbs
5. Reset to the bottom of the rep range with the new weight and repeat.

### RIR Targets by Week Within Each Mesocycle

| Week in Meso | RIR Target | Effort Level |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 (intro) | 3-4 RIR | Conservative -- focus on form and groove |
| Week 2 (build) | 2-3 RIR | Moderate -- pushing but controlled |
| Week 3 (push) | 1-2 RIR | Hard -- approaching failure on isolations |
| Week 4 (deload) | 5+ RIR | Easy -- 50% of volume, same loads, recovery focus |

### Volume Progression Strategy

- Each working week within a mesocycle adds 1-2 sets per muscle group compared to the previous week.
- Sets are added by: (a) adding a set to an existing exercise, or (b) converting a straight set to a myo-rep finisher.
- Deload weeks cut volume to 50% (roughly half the sets) while keeping the same loads.
- Each new mesocycle restarts volume at the previous mesocycle's Week 2 level, then ramps higher.

### Warm-Up Protocol (Every Session)

**General Warm-Up (5 minutes):**
- 2 min light cardio (bike, rowing machine, or brisk walk)
- 1 min arm circles + band pull-aparts (upper days) OR bodyweight squats + leg swings (lower days)
- 1 min dynamic stretching targeting the session's primary muscles
- 1 min foam rolling on any tight areas

**Specific Warm-Up Sets (before each compound exercise):**
- Set 1: Empty bar or 40% working weight x 10 reps
- Set 2: 60% working weight x 5 reps
- Set 3: 80% working weight x 3 reps
- Then begin working sets

Isolation exercises do not need specific warm-up sets if the target muscle is already warm from compounds.

### Deload Week Instructions (Weeks 4, 8, 12)

- Perform 50% of the working sets (round up if odd number). Example: if Week 3 has 5 sets of bench, do 3 sets during deload.
- Keep the SAME loads you used in Week 3. Do not reduce weight.
- All sets at 5+ RIR. Stop well short of failure. The point is recovery, not stimulus.
- Keep the same exercises and order. Do not add exercises or substitute harder variations.
- Skip myo-rep sets entirely. Replace with a single straight set of 8-10 reps.
- Session duration should be 35-45 minutes.
- Focus on sleep, nutrition, and mobility work during deload weeks.
- Week 12 deload: optionally test new rep maxes on the main compounds (squat, bench, OHP, RDL) on the last set.

### Rest Period Guide

| Exercise Type | Rest Between Sets |
|---|---|
| Explosive work (med ball, box jumps) | 90 seconds |
| Heavy compounds (squat, bench, RDL, OHP) -- standalone | 2-3 minutes |
| Heavy compounds -- in a superset | 60s after first exercise, 90s after second (before next round) |
| Secondary compounds (incline press, rows, lunges, pulldowns) | 90-120 seconds |
| Isolation exercises | 60-90 seconds |
| Supersets -- between exercises in the pair | 0 seconds (go immediately) |
| Supersets -- between rounds | 60-90 seconds after the second exercise |

## Post-Program: What Next?

After completing the 12-week program:

1. **Take a full rest week** (Week 13) -- light walks, stretching, no lifting.
2. **Review your training log.** Compare Week 1 loads to Week 11 loads. You should see +10-20% on compounds and +5-15 lbs on most isolations.
3. **Re-run the program** with new starting loads (your Week 11 weights become your new baseline), or transition to a different mesocycle structure (e.g., push/pull/legs if you want higher frequency for lagging muscles).
4. **Adjust volume starting points** based on what you learned. If you were recovering fine at Week 11, start your next Meso 1 at higher volumes. If you were crushed, dial back.
