## UPPER A -- Horizontal Push/Pull (Monday)

### Med Ball Chest Pass (Explosive)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Chest (pectoralis major), front deltoids
- Secondary: Triceps, core (stabilizers)

![Med Ball Chest Pass](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Chest_Push_single_response/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Chest_Push_single_response (closest match in free-exercise-db) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, holding the ball at chest height
2. Explosively extend arms and push the ball forward from the chest -- drive through the hands
3. Follow through with arms fully extended; absorb the return catch with soft elbows

**Common Mistakes**
- Throwing from overhead instead of chest level, turning it into a slam rather than a press
- Standing flat-footed with no athletic stance -- reduces power transfer from the ground up
- Losing core bracing on release, causing the lower back to hyperextend

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (power primers), Jeremy Ethier (explosive warm-up series)

**Alternatives**
1. Explosive push-ups (clap)
2. Banded chest press throws

### Barbell Bench Press

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Pectoralis major (mid-chest emphasis), anterior deltoid
- Secondary: Triceps (lateral + medial heads), serratus anterior

![Barbell Bench Press](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Bench_Press_-_Medium_Grip/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Retract and depress scapulae ("shoulder blades in back pockets") before unracking
2. Bar touches lower chest, elbows at 45-75 degrees (never flare to 90)
3. Drive feet into floor, press back toward rack following a J-curve bar path

**Common Mistakes**
- Flaring elbows to 90 degrees, placing excessive stress on the shoulder joint
- Losing scapular retraction mid-set, causing the shoulders to protract and take over
- Bouncing the bar off the chest instead of controlling the eccentric

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (bench press technique), RP (chest training tips), Jeremy Ethier (bench fix video)

**Alternatives**
1. Dumbbell bench press
2. Machine chest press

---

### Barbell Bent-Over Row

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Latissimus dorsi (thickness), rhomboids, mid/lower trapezius
- Secondary: Rear deltoids, biceps, erector spinae (isometric)

![Barbell Row](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Bent_Over_Barbell_Row/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Maintain a 45-degree torso angle with a flat back throughout the movement
2. Pull bar to lower chest/upper abdomen, driving elbows behind you
3. Control the eccentric -- no bouncing or using momentum at the bottom

**Common Mistakes**
- Excessive torso rise (body English), turning the row into a shrug/hip extension hybrid
- Shrugging the shoulders instead of rowing -- traps dominate, lats underworked
- Rounding the lower back under load, risking disc injury

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (back thickness), RP (row technique), Jeremy Ethier (back exercises ranked)

**Alternatives**
1. Dumbbell bent-over row
2. T-bar row

### Incline Dumbbell Press (30 degrees)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Pectoralis major (upper/clavicular head -- EMG-optimized at 30 degrees)
- Secondary: Anterior deltoid, triceps

![Incline Dumbbell Press](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Incline_Bench_Press_-_Medium_Grip/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Barbell_Incline_Bench_Press (closest available; dumbbell variant not in repo) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Set bench to 30 degrees (not 45) -- EMG data shows best upper chest activation at 30
2. Retract scapulae before pressing, maintain arch; dumbbells travel in a slight arc
3. At the bottom, forearms should be vertical; press up and slightly inward without clanking dumbbells

**Common Mistakes**
- Setting the bench too steep (45+ degrees), which shifts load to the front delts
- Letting dumbbells drift too far toward the shoulders at the bottom, losing chest stretch
- Pressing dumbbells straight up instead of slightly converging, missing the adduction component

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (upper chest), Jeremy Ethier (incline press angle study), RP (chest volume guide)

**Alternatives**
1. Incline barbell press
2. Low-to-high cable fly

### Seated Cable Row

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Latissimus dorsi (thickness), rhomboids, mid trapezius
- Secondary: Rear deltoids, biceps, erector spinae (isometric)

![Seated Cable Row](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Seated_Cable_Rows/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Seated_Cable_Rows (expected; may need verification) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Sit tall with a slight forward lean at start; pull handle to lower chest, squeezing shoulder blades together
2. Keep chest up and avoid rounding the upper back -- torso stays mostly stationary
3. Control the eccentric, allowing a full stretch with arms extended without losing spinal position

**Common Mistakes**
- Excessive torso swing back and forth, turning it into a momentum-driven movement
- Pulling too high (toward neck) which shifts load to upper traps and rear delts away from lats
- Shrugging at the top instead of depressing and retracting the scapulae

**Video Reference**
Watch: RP (back width vs thickness), Jeff Nippard (cable row variations), Jeremy Ethier (back guide)

**Alternatives**
1. Chest-supported T-bar row
2. Single-arm dumbbell row

### Dumbbell Lateral Raise

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Lateral (medial) deltoid -- EMG shows highest activation with slight lean
- Secondary: Anterior deltoid, upper trapezius (if form breaks down)

![Lateral Raise](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Side_Lateral_Raise/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Side_Lateral_Raise -->

**Form Cues**
1. Keep shoulders depressed ("away from ears") -- if traps take over, the weight is too heavy
2. Lead with elbows, maintain a slight bend; raise to shoulder height only, not above
3. Slight forward lean aligns the lateral delt fiber direction better with gravity

**Common Mistakes**
- Shrugging the traps to initiate the lift, stealing tension from the lateral delt
- Using momentum and swinging the weights up -- kills time-under-tension
- Raising above shoulder height which engages traps more than lateral delts

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (lateral raise deep dive), RP (delt training), Jeremy Ethier (shoulder exercises ranked)

**Alternatives**
1. Cable lateral raise (constant tension throughout ROM)
2. Machine lateral raise

### Face Pulls

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Rear deltoid, infraspinatus, teres minor (external rotators)
- Secondary: Rhomboids, mid trapezius

![Face Pulls](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Face_Pull/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Set cable at face height with rope attachment -- stand with slight backward lean
2. Pull toward face while externally rotating -- finish with hands beside ears, thumbs pointing back
3. Keep elbows high (at or above shoulder height) throughout the entire movement

**Common Mistakes**
- Setting the cable too low or too high, changing the line of pull away from rear delts
- Pulling to the chest instead of the face, losing the external rotation component
- Using too much weight, causing the body to lean forward and turning it into a row

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (rear delts), Jeremy Ethier (face pull tutorial), Squat University (shoulder health)

**Alternatives**
1. Reverse pec deck
2. Band pull-aparts

### Barbell Curl

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Biceps brachii (short head emphasis with standard grip)
- Secondary: Brachialis, brachioradialis, forearm flexors

![Barbell Curl](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Curl/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Pin elbows at sides -- no forward drift or shoulder involvement
2. Full ROM: complete extension at bottom, full squeeze at top with a brief hold
3. Control the eccentric for 2-3 seconds -- the negative is where growth happens

**Common Mistakes**
- Swinging the torso back to generate momentum, especially on the last few reps
- Elbows drifting forward mid-curl, which reduces tension on the biceps
- Cutting ROM short at the bottom, missing the fully lengthened position

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (bicep curl breakdown), RP (arm training), Jeremy Ethier (bicep mistakes)

**Alternatives**
1. EZ-bar curl (easier on wrists)
2. Dumbbell alternating curl

### Tricep Pushdown (Rope)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Triceps brachii (lateral head emphasis with V-bar/rope per EMG data)
- Secondary: Triceps medial head, anconeus

![Tricep Pushdown](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Triceps_Pushdown/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Triceps_Pushdown (expected) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Pin elbows to sides -- if shoulders move, the lats are taking over
2. Full lockout at bottom with a hard squeeze; spread the rope ends apart at peak contraction
3. Control the return phase -- do not let the stack yank your hands up

**Common Mistakes**
- Elbows flaring out or drifting forward, incorporating the chest and shoulders
- Leaning excessively over the cable, turning pushdowns into a chest press movement
- Using too much weight and cutting ROM short at full extension

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (tricep pushdown variations), RP (tricep training guide), Jeremy Ethier (arm day)

**Alternatives**
1. Overhead cable tricep extension (targets long head)
2. Dips (bodyweight or machine)

## LOWER A -- Quad Emphasis (Tuesday)

### Box Jumps (Explosive)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Quadriceps, glutes, calves (plyometric power)
- Secondary: Hamstrings, hip flexors, core (stabilizers)

![Box Jumps](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Box_Jump_Multiple_Response/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Box_Jump_Multiple_Response -->

**Form Cues**
1. Start in an athletic stance, countermovement with arms swinging back before the jump
2. Explode upward through full hip/knee/ankle extension -- land softly on the box with both feet
3. Stand tall on top of the box, then step down (do not jump down to save your knees)

**Common Mistakes**
- Jumping down from the box instead of stepping down, adding unnecessary joint impact
- Landing in a deep squat on the box -- the goal is hip height, not knee tuck height
- Choosing a box too high and compensating with excessive knee tuck rather than actual jump height

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (plyometric warm-ups), Squat University (box jump safety), Jeremy Ethier (athletic training)

**Alternatives**
1. Squat jumps (no box needed)
2. Broad jumps

### Barbell Back Squat

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Quadriceps (VMO/inner quad at full depth per EMG), gluteus maximus
- Secondary: Adductors, hamstrings, erector spinae, core

![Barbell Squat](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Squat/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Brace core hard -- breathe into belly and obliques before descending
2. Push knees out over toes, never let them cave inward
3. Break at hips and knees simultaneously, sit "between" your legs to depth

**Common Mistakes**
- Knees caving inward (valgus collapse), especially coming out of the hole
- Rising hips-first (good-morning squat), shifting the load to the lower back
- Insufficient depth -- cutting above parallel misses glute and VMO activation

**Video Reference**
Watch: Squat University (squat mobility + form), Jeff Nippard (squat technique), RP (quad training)

**Alternatives**
1. Goblet squat
2. Front squat

### Leg Press

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Quadriceps, gluteus maximus
- Secondary: Adductors, hamstrings (depending on foot placement)

![Leg Press](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Leg_Press/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Leg_Press (expected) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Place feet shoulder-width on the platform, midfoot position; higher = more glute, lower = more quad
2. Lower the sled until knees reach 90 degrees -- do not let the lower back round off the pad
3. Press through the full foot, driving evenly; do not lock out the knees at the top (soft lockout)

**Common Mistakes**
- Allowing the lower back to round by going too deep -- the hips lift off the seat
- Placing feet too low and too close together, putting excessive stress on the knees
- Locking out knees fully at the top, risking hyperextension under heavy load

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (leg press guide), RP (quad exercises ranked), Jeremy Ethier (leg day)

**Alternatives**
1. Hack squat machine
2. Belt squat

### Walking Lunges (Dumbbell)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Quadriceps, gluteus maximus
- Secondary: Hamstrings, adductors, calves, core (balance stabilizers)

![Walking Lunges](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Walking_Lunge/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Take a moderate stride -- too short overloads the knee, too long overstretches the hip flexor
2. Front shin stays roughly vertical at the bottom; knee tracks over the 2nd/3rd toe
3. Push off the front foot to step through; keep torso upright with dumbbells at sides

**Common Mistakes**
- Leaning the torso forward excessively, shifting the load off the quads and onto the lower back
- Taking steps that are too narrow (feet in a line), causing lateral instability
- Letting the back knee slam into the floor instead of lowering under control

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (lunge variations), Jeremy Ethier (leg training), RP (glute/quad volume)

**Alternatives**
1. Reverse lunges (stationary)
2. Step-ups (dumbbell or bodyweight)

### Leg Curl (Lying or Seated)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Hamstrings (biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus)
- Secondary: Gastrocnemius (assists knee flexion), popliteus

![Leg Curl](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Seated_Leg_Curl/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Seated_Leg_Curl (expected) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Adjust the pad to sit just above the ankle (not on the calf belly); secure the thigh pad
2. Curl the weight fully, squeezing the hamstrings hard at peak contraction
3. Control the eccentric over 2-3 seconds -- do not let the weight slam back

**Common Mistakes**
- Lifting the hips off the pad during the curl (lying version), using hip flexion to cheat
- Using too much weight and relying on momentum instead of hamstring contraction
- Cutting ROM short by not fully extending at the bottom or not fully curling at the top

**Video Reference**
Watch: RP (hamstring training), Jeff Nippard (hamstring exercises ranked), Jeremy Ethier (leg curl tips)

**Alternatives**
1. Nordic hamstring curl (bodyweight)
2. Swiss ball leg curl

### Leg Extension

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Quadriceps (all four heads; full-ROM hits VMO per EMG data)
- Secondary: Tibialis anterior (minor stabilizer)

![Leg Extension](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Leg_Extensions/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Leg_Extensions -->

**Form Cues**
1. Adjust the pad to sit just above the ankles; back flat against the seat
2. Extend fully to lockout -- the VMO fires hardest in the last 30 degrees of extension
3. Lower under control; pause briefly at the top with a hard quad squeeze

**Common Mistakes**
- Using momentum by swinging the legs up, which removes constant tension from the quads
- Not reaching full extension, missing peak quad contraction
- Setting the machine seat too far back, causing the knee joint to be behind the pivot point

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (quad training), RP (leg extension technique), Jeremy Ethier (quad exercises)

**Alternatives**
1. Sissy squat
2. Single-leg leg extension (reduced load option)

### Standing Calf Raise

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Gastrocnemius (both heads -- straight-knee position emphasizes gastroc per EMG)
- Secondary: Soleus (minor role when knee is straight)

![Standing Calf Raise](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Standing_Calf_Raises/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Standing_Calf_Raises (expected) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Full stretch at the bottom -- let the heels drop below the platform for a deep stretch
2. Drive up onto the balls of the feet, pause and squeeze at the peak for 1-2 seconds
3. Control the eccentric for 2-3 seconds; do not bounce at the bottom

**Common Mistakes**
- Bouncing with no pause at the top, using the Achilles tendon stretch reflex instead of muscle
- Insufficient stretch at the bottom -- cutting ROM costs the most productive range
- Bending the knees during the movement, which shifts load to the soleus

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (calf training), RP (calf growth guide), Jeremy Ethier (stubborn calves)

**Alternatives**
1. Smith machine calf raise
2. Donkey calf raise

### Hanging Leg Raise

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Rectus abdominis (lower emphasis), hip flexors (psoas, iliacus)
- Secondary: Obliques (with rotation variant), forearm flexors (grip)

![Hanging Leg Raise](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Hanging_Leg_Raise/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Hanging_Leg_Raise (expected) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Hang from a bar with arms fully extended; depress the shoulders slightly
2. Raise legs by curling the pelvis upward -- focus on posterior pelvic tilt, not just hip flexion
3. Control the descent; do not swing -- if swinging occurs, bend knees to reduce the lever arm

**Common Mistakes**
- Only lifting the legs without tilting the pelvis, which works hip flexors but barely engages abs
- Using momentum and swinging back and forth -- kills ab tension
- Losing grip before abs fatigue; use straps if grip is the limiting factor

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (ab training), RP (ab exercise guide), Jeremy Ethier (six-pack exercises ranked)

**Alternatives**
1. Captain's chair leg raise
2. Lying leg raise (on bench)

### Explosive Push-ups (Clap)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, triceps (power output)
- Secondary: Core (stabilizers), serratus anterior

![Explosive Push-ups](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Push-Up_Wide/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Push-Up_Wide (closest general push-up in repo) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Start in a standard push-up position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width
2. Lower with control, then explode upward with enough force for hands to leave the ground
3. Land softly with elbows slightly bent to absorb impact -- immediately flow into the next rep

**Common Mistakes**
- Sagging the hips (losing core bracing), turning the movement into a spinal extension exercise
- Not generating enough explosive force -- the push-up becomes a regular slow rep
- Landing with locked elbows, jarring the wrist and elbow joints

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (push-up variations), Jeremy Ethier (bodyweight power), RP (explosive training)

**Alternatives**
1. Med ball chest pass
2. Plyometric incline push-ups (hands on bench for reduced difficulty)

### Overhead Press (Barbell)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid
- Secondary: Triceps (long + lateral heads), upper trapezius, serratus anterior, core

![Overhead Press](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Shoulder_Press/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Squeeze glutes and brace core to prevent lower back hyperextension
2. Press bar in a straight line -- move head back slightly, then push "through the window"
3. Bar starts on front delts/clavicle, not floating in front of the body

**Common Mistakes**
- Excessive back arch (turning the OHP into a standing incline press), risking lumbar injury
- Pressing the bar around the head in a curved path instead of straight up
- Not engaging the core, allowing rib flare and energy leaks

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (OHP technique), RP (delt training), Jeremy Ethier (shoulder press guide)

**Alternatives**
1. Dumbbell shoulder press (seated or standing)
2. Landmine press

### Pull-ups (Weighted if Possible)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Latissimus dorsi (width -- vertical pull per EMG), teres major
- Secondary: Biceps, brachialis, rhomboids, lower trapezius

![Pull-ups](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Pullups/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Pullups (expected) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Depress scapulae first ("shoulder blades down") before bending the elbows
2. Pull elbows down and slightly back toward the hips -- think "pull the bar to your chest"
3. Full stretch at the bottom (dead hang), full contraction at the top -- no partial ROM

**Common Mistakes**
- Kipping or using momentum to get the chin over the bar instead of controlled pulling
- Not reaching full extension at the bottom, missing the lat stretch
- Shrugging the shoulders at the top instead of keeping them depressed and packed

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (pull-up progression), RP (back width exercises), Jeremy Ethier (pull-up guide)

**Alternatives**
1. Lat pulldown (same motor pattern, adjustable load)
2. Band-assisted pull-ups

### Cable Fly (Low-to-High)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Pectoralis major (upper/clavicular head -- EMG-optimized for upper chest)
- Secondary: Anterior deltoid, biceps (stabilizer)

![Cable Fly](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Cable_Crossover/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Cable_Crossover (closest to cable fly in repo) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Set pulleys at the lowest position; step forward with a staggered stance for stability
2. Bring handles from low to high in a hugging arc, finishing at upper chest/chin height
3. Squeeze at the top for a full second; control the eccentric back to a full stretch

**Common Mistakes**
- Bending the elbows excessively, turning the fly into a pressing movement
- Not setting pulleys low enough -- if pulleys are at mid-height, the upper chest emphasis is lost
- Using too much weight, losing the mind-muscle connection and compensating with shoulders

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (upper chest cable fly), RP (chest training), Jeremy Ethier (cable exercises)

**Alternatives**
1. Incline dumbbell fly
2. Incline dumbbell press (30 degrees)

### Lat Pulldown (Wide Grip)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Latissimus dorsi (width -- EMG confirms vertical pulling for lat width)
- Secondary: Teres major, biceps, brachialis, lower trapezius, rhomboids

![Lat Pulldown](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Full_Range-Of-Motion_Lat_Pulldown/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Full_Range-Of-Motion_Lat_Pulldown -->

**Form Cues**
1. Depress scapulae ("shoulder blades down") before bending the elbows
2. Pull the bar to upper chest, driving elbows down and slightly back
3. Full stretch at the top -- let the weight pull the shoulders up for a complete lat stretch

**Common Mistakes**
- Leaning too far back, turning the pulldown into a row (limits lat stretch)
- Pulling the bar behind the neck, which externally rotates the shoulder under load (injury risk)
- Using momentum by rocking the torso -- the weight should move, not the body

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (lat pulldown deep dive), RP (back width guide), Jeremy Ethier (lat exercises ranked)

**Alternatives**
1. Pull-ups (bodyweight or band-assisted)
2. Close-grip pulldown (shifts emphasis slightly to lower lats)

### Cable Lateral Raise

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Lateral (medial) deltoid -- EMG shows constant tension advantage over dumbbells
- Secondary: Anterior deltoid, supraspinatus (initiation)

**Form Cues**

**Common Mistakes**

- Standing too close to the cable, creating a poor resistance curve
- Using body sway to initiate the rep instead of isolating the lateral delt

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (cable vs dumbbell laterals), RP (lateral delt guide), Jeremy Ethier (delt exercises)

**Alternatives**
1. Dumbbell lateral raise (with slight lean)
2. Machine lateral raise

### Rear Delt Fly (Incline Bench)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Posterior (rear) deltoid -- EMG shows high activation with incline support
- Secondary: Rhomboids, mid trapezius, infraspinatus

![Rear Delt Fly](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Dumbbell_Lying_Rear_Lateral_Raise/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Dumbbell_Lying_Rear_Lateral_Raise -->

**Form Cues**
1. Lie chest-down on an incline bench (30-45 degrees) with dumbbells hanging below
2. Raise dumbbells out to the sides with a slight elbow bend, squeezing the rear delts at the top
3. Lead with the elbows, not the hands; keep the wrists neutral (do not rotate)

**Common Mistakes**
- Using too much weight and turning it into a row by bending the elbows excessively
- Not using the incline bench (standing bent-over version allows too much momentum cheating)
- Shrugging the traps at the top instead of keeping scapulae neutral

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (rear delt training), RP (rear delt exercises ranked), Jeremy Ethier (rear delts)

**Alternatives**
1. Reverse pec deck machine
2. Cable rear delt fly

### Incline Dumbbell Curl

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Biceps brachii long head (peak) -- EMG shows highest long head activation with shoulder extended
- Secondary: Brachialis, brachioradialis

![Incline Dumbbell Curl](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Alternate_Incline_Dumbbell_Curl/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Set bench to 45-60 degrees; let arms hang straight down with palms forward (shoulder extended)
2. Curl without moving the upper arm -- elbows stay pinned behind the torso
3. Full stretch at the bottom (feel the bicep lengthen), controlled squeeze at the top

**Common Mistakes**
- Moving the elbows forward during the curl, shortening the long head's stretched position
- Using too much weight and swinging the dumbbells instead of curling under control
- Setting the bench too upright (defeats the purpose -- the incline creates the shoulder extension)

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (bicep long head), RP (arm training), Jeremy Ethier (bicep peak exercises)

**Alternatives**
1. Behind-the-body cable curl (drag curl)
2. Preacher curl (emphasizes short head instead)

### Overhead Tricep Extension (Cable)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Triceps brachii long head -- EMG shows highest long head activation in the stretched overhead position
- Secondary: Triceps medial head, anconeus

![Overhead Tricep Extension](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Cable_Rope_Overhead_Triceps_Extension/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Cable_Rope_Overhead_Triceps_Extension -->

**Form Cues**
1. Face away from the cable (low pulley), rope overhead; step forward into a staggered stance
2. Extend the arms fully overhead without moving the upper arms -- only the forearms move
3. Get a full stretch at the bottom (deep elbow flexion behind the head), squeeze at lockout

**Common Mistakes**
- Flaring the elbows out wide, reducing long head tension and stressing the shoulder joint
- Arching the lower back to compensate for tight shoulders or heavy weight
- Using too much weight and moving the entire arm instead of hinging at the elbow only

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (tricep long head), RP (tricep exercises ranked), Jeremy Ethier (tricep training)

**Alternatives**
1. Dumbbell overhead extension (single or double arm)
2. Skull crushers (EZ-bar)

### Broad Jumps (Explosive)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Quadriceps, glutes, calves (horizontal power output)
- Secondary: Hamstrings, core, hip flexors

![Broad Jumps](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Frog_Hops/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Frog_Hops (closest horizontal jump pattern in repo) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Start in an athletic stance, feet shoulder-width; countermovement with arms swinging back
2. Explode forward and upward, extending hips/knees/ankles fully at takeoff
3. Land softly with knees bent, absorbing the landing -- stick the landing before the next rep

**Common Mistakes**
- Landing with straight legs, sending impact through the knees and spine
- Not using a full arm swing, which reduces horizontal distance and power
- Prioritizing distance over landing quality, causing poor deceleration mechanics

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (plyometric training), Jeremy Ethier (athletic power), Squat University (jump mechanics)

**Alternatives**
1. Box jumps
2. Squat jumps

### Romanian Deadlift

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Hamstrings (biceps femoris, semitendinosus), gluteus maximus
- Secondary: Erector spinae (isometric), adductors, forearm flexors (grip)

![Romanian Deadlift](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Romanian_Deadlift_With_Dumbbells/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Romanian_Deadlift_With_Dumbbells (barbell variant may also exist) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Neutral spine throughout -- NEVER round the lower back at any point
2. Push hips back (hip hinge); hamstring stretch dictates depth, not a target position
3. Bar stays in contact with thighs and shins -- maintain a vertical bar path

**Common Mistakes**
- Rounding the lower back, especially at heavier weights, risking disc herniation
- Bar drifting away from the body, increasing the moment arm and stressing the lower back
- Bending the knees too much, turning the RDL into a conventional deadlift

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (RDL technique), RP (hamstring training), Jeremy Ethier (RDL vs conventional)

**Alternatives**
1. Dumbbell Romanian deadlift
2. Single-leg Romanian deadlift

### Bulgarian Split Squat (Dumbbell)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Quadriceps, gluteus maximus (front leg dominant)
- Secondary: Adductors, hamstrings, calves, core (balance)

![Bulgarian Split Squat](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Dumbbell_Lunges/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Dumbbell_Lunges (closest single-leg pattern available) -->

**Form Cues**
1. Rear foot elevated on a bench (laces down); front foot 2-3 feet ahead of the bench
2. Descend until the front thigh is parallel or just below; front shin stays roughly vertical
3. Drive up through the front heel; keep torso upright with dumbbells at sides

**Common Mistakes**
- Standing too close to the bench, causing the front knee to travel excessively forward
- Leaning the torso forward, which shifts load from quads to lower back
- Placing the rear foot too centrally (in line with front foot), causing lateral instability

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (BSS guide), RP (quad exercises), Jeremy Ethier (single-leg training)

**Alternatives**
1. Reverse lunge (foot on floor)
2. Step-ups (dumbbell)

### Seated Leg Curl

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Hamstrings (biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus)
- Secondary: Gastrocnemius (assists knee flexion), popliteus

![Seated Leg Curl](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Seated_Leg_Curl/0.jpg)
<!-- Slug: Seated_Leg_Curl -->

**Form Cues**
1. Adjust the thigh pad snugly above the knees; ankle pad just above the Achilles tendon
2. Curl the weight fully with a 1-second squeeze at peak contraction
3. Control the eccentric for 2-3 seconds -- do not let the weight slam back to start

**Common Mistakes**
- Lifting the hips off the seat to generate momentum
- Not achieving full knee flexion, cutting the contraction short
- Going too heavy and using body English instead of hamstring contraction

**Video Reference**
Watch: RP (hamstring training), Jeff Nippard (hamstring exercises), Jeremy Ethier (leg curl form)

**Alternatives**
1. Lying leg curl
2. Nordic hamstring curl (bodyweight)

### Hip Thrust (Barbell)

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Gluteus maximus (highest EMG activation of any glute exercise)
- Secondary: Hamstrings, quadriceps (minor), adductors, core

![Hip Thrust](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Hip_Thrust/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Upper back on a bench, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart; barbell over the hip crease with a pad
2. Drive hips up by squeezing glutes -- finish with hips fully extended, torso parallel to the floor
3. Chin tucked throughout to prevent cervical hyperextension; ribs down, do not flare

**Common Mistakes**
- Hyperextending the lower back at lockout instead of stopping at full hip extension
- Placing feet too far forward (hamstring dominant) or too close (quad dominant)
- Not using a barbell pad, causing hip bone discomfort that limits load progression

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (glute training), RP (hip thrust technique), Jeremy Ethier (glute exercises ranked)

**Alternatives**
1. Glute bridge (barbell, on floor)
2. Cable pull-through

### Seated Calf Raise

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Soleus (bent-knee position per EMG de-emphasizes gastrocnemius, isolating soleus)
- Secondary: Gastrocnemius (minor role when knee is bent)

![Seated Calf Raise](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Barbell_Seated_Calf_Raise/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Sit with the pad snug on the lower thighs; balls of the feet on the platform edge
2. Full stretch at the bottom -- let heels drop below the platform for maximum ROM
3. Drive up onto the balls of the feet, pause and squeeze at the top for 1-2 seconds

**Common Mistakes**
- Bouncing with no pause at the top, relying on tendon recoil instead of muscle contraction
- Insufficient stretch at the bottom -- the soleus responds best to full ROM
- Using too much weight and compensating with tiny range of motion

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (calf training science), RP (calf training), Jeremy Ethier (calf growth)

**Alternatives**
1. Smith machine seated calf raise (plate on knees)
2. Single-leg seated calf raise (bodyweight on one knee)

---

### Cable Crunch

**Target Muscles**
- Primary: Rectus abdominis (upper emphasis with spinal flexion under load)
- Secondary: Obliques (internal + external), hip flexors (minor)

![Cable Crunch](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yuhonas/free-exercise-db/main/exercises/Cable_Crunch/0.jpg)

**Form Cues**
1. Kneel facing away from the cable (high pulley), rope behind the head; hips locked in position
2. Crunch by flexing the spine -- bring elbows toward the knees by rounding the torso, not hinging at the hips
3. Squeeze at the bottom (peak contraction), then control the eccentric back to the stretched position

**Common Mistakes**
- Hinging at the hips instead of flexing the spine -- this works the hip flexors, not the abs
- Pulling the rope with the arms instead of using the abdominals to curl the torso
- Sitting back onto the heels during the crunch, reducing abdominal tension

**Video Reference**
Watch: Jeff Nippard (ab training), RP (cable crunch tutorial), Jeremy Ethier (ab exercises ranked)

**Alternatives**
1. Ab wheel rollout
2. Hanging leg raise
