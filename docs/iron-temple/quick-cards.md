## PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD CHEAT SHEET

### When to Increase Weight

You increase the load when you hit the **top of the rep range on ALL prescribed sets** at your target RIR.

**Example -- Bench Press (4x6-10):**
- Week 1: 185x8, 185x8, 185x7, 185x6 -- NOT yet (sets 3-4 below 10)
- Week 2: 185x10, 185x10, 185x9, 185x8 -- NOT yet (sets 3-4 below 10)
- Week 3: 185x10, 185x10, 185x10, 185x10 -- YES, increase next session

### How Much to Increase

| Exercise Type | Increase | Examples |
|---------------|----------|----------|
| Heavy compounds | +5-10 lbs | Squat, Bench, Row, RDL, OHP |
| Light compounds | +5 lbs | Incline DB Press, Lunges, Hip Thrust |
| Isolation | +2.5-5 lbs | Curls, Laterals, Extensions, Pushdowns |
| Cable isolation | +1 plate (5-10 lbs) | Cable Fly, Cable Lateral, Face Pulls |

After increasing, you reset to the **bottom of the rep range** and build back up.

### What RIR Means

**RIR = Reps In Reserve** -- how many more reps you COULD have done with good form.

| RIR | What It Feels Like | Bar Speed |
|-----|---------------------|-----------|
| 4 | Easy, could do many more | Fast, no grind |
| 3 | Moderate, clearly 3+ left | Smooth, maybe slight slowdown |
| 2 | Hard, definitely only 2 left | Noticeable slowdown on last rep |
| 1 | Very hard, only 1 more possible | Last rep is slow/grinding |
| 0 | Failure -- could not do one more | Rep barely completed or failed |

**Practical test:** If someone offered you $100 to do one more rep, could you? That is 1 RIR. Could you do two more for $100 each? That is 2 RIR.

**RIR across the mesocycle:**
- Week 1: 3-4 RIR (building, leaving a lot in the tank)
- Week 2: 2-3 RIR (moderate effort)
- Week 3: 1-2 RIR (pushing hard, close to failure)
- Week 4: DELOAD -- 5+ RIR (very light, recovery week)

### Deload Week Rules

Deload happens every 4th week (Weeks 4, 8, 12).

| Parameter | Deload Protocol |
|-----------|----------------|
| Volume | Cut to 50% (halve the number of sets) |
| Load | Keep the SAME weight as Week 3 |
| RIR | 5+ (every set should feel easy) |
| Myo-rep sets | Skip entirely |
| Explosive work | Reduce to 1 set or skip |
| Session time | ~35-45 min |

**Purpose:** Dissipate fatigue, allow tendons/joints to recover, reset sensitivity to training stimulus. You do NOT lose muscle or strength in one easy week -- you come back stronger.

**Signs you need an unplanned deload:**
- Lifts stalling or regressing for 2+ sessions
- Persistent joint pain (not muscle soreness)
- Sleep quality tanking despite no life changes
- Motivation and energy consistently low
- Getting sick more often

### Myo-Rep Set Protocol

1. Do an **activation set** of 12-15 reps to near failure (0-1 RIR)
2. Rest **5 deep breaths** (~15-20 seconds)
3. Do a **mini-set** of 3-5 reps
4. Rest 5 breaths again
5. Repeat mini-sets until you can no longer hit 3 reps
6. Typically yields 3-5 mini-sets

**Why:** Accumulates effective reps (the growth-stimulating reps near failure) in less time than traditional straight sets.
