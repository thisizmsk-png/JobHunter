## Meal Prep Sunday -- Step-by-Step

Block 2.5-3 hours on Sunday afternoon. This covers weekday lunches and partially pre-cooks dinners.

### What You Are Prepping

| Item | Quantity | Used For |
|------|----------|----------|
| Tandoori/tikka chicken | 2 kg (4.4 lbs) raw breast | Lunches Mon-Fri, Dinner Sun |
| Large pot of dal (toor or masoor) | 3-4 cups dry = ~8 cups cooked | Lunches + dinner sides |
| Brown/basmati rice | 4-5 cups dry = ~10 cups cooked | Lunches all week |
| Goat keema | 400g (14 oz) | Monday dinner, Friday lunch |
| Hard-boiled eggs | 12 eggs | Snacks throughout week |
| Palak sabzi | 1 large batch (500g spinach) | Dinner sides |

### Step-by-Step Instructions

**Step 1: Start the Dal (5 min active, 30 min passive)**

1. Rinse 2 cups toor dal + 1 cup masoor dal in water until clear.
2. Add to a large pot with 6 cups water, 1/2 tsp turmeric, and 1 tsp salt.
3. Bring to boil, then reduce to medium-low. Cover and simmer 25-30 min until soft.
4. Meanwhile, in a small pan heat 1 tbsp ghee. Add 1 tsp cumin seeds, let splutter. Add 1/2 tsp red chili powder, 2 chopped tomatoes, pinch of hing. Cook 3 min. Pour this tadka into the dal. Stir and set aside.

**Step 2: Marinate the Chicken (10 min active, 60 min passive)**

1. Cut 2 kg chicken breast into tikka-sized pieces (2-inch cubes) and larger breast halves for tandoori.
2. In a large bowl, mix: 1 cup dahi (yogurt), 3 tbsp tandoori masala, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder, juice of 1 lemon, 1 tsp salt.
3. Coat all chicken thoroughly. Cover and refrigerate for minimum 1 hour (overnight is better -- marinate Saturday night if possible).

**Step 3: Cook Rice (5 min active, 20 min passive)**

1. Rinse 4 cups basmati rice until water runs clear.
2. Add to rice cooker or pot with 6 cups water and 1/2 tsp salt.
3. Cook until done (~18-20 min). Fluff with fork and let cool.

**Step 4: Hard Boil Eggs (5 min active, 15 min passive)**

1. Place 12 eggs in a pot, cover with cold water by 1 inch.
2. Bring to rolling boil, then turn off heat. Cover, let sit 10 minutes.
3. Transfer to ice water bath for 5 min. Peel and store in container.

**Step 5: Cook the Chicken (10 min active, 25 min passive)**

1. Preheat oven to 425F (220C). Line 2 baking sheets with foil, lightly oil.
2. Spread marinated chicken in single layer on sheets. Do not crowd.
3. Bake 20-25 min until internal temp reaches 165F (74C) and edges are lightly charred.
4. For extra char: broil on high for 2-3 min at the end (watch carefully).
5. Let cool 10 min before portioning.

**Step 6: Cook Goat Keema (10 min active, 25 min passive)**

1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a heavy pan on medium-high.
2. Add 1 diced onion, cook 5 min until golden.
3. Add 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, cook 1 min.
4. Add 400g goat keema mince, break apart, cook 5-7 min until browned.
5. Add 1 diced tomato, 1 tsp garam masala, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp red chili, salt. Cook 10-15 min on medium until dry and fragrant.
6. Garnish with fresh coriander. Let cool.

**Step 7: Make Palak Sabzi (10 min active, 10 min passive)**

1. Blanch 500g fresh spinach in boiling water for 2 min. Drain and puree roughly.
2. In a pan, heat 1 tbsp ghee. Add 1 tsp cumin, 1 chopped onion, 2 cloves garlic. Cook 3 min.
3. Add pureed spinach, 1/2 tsp garam masala, salt. Cook 5-7 min.
4. Set aside to cool.

**Step 8: Portion and Store**

1. Get 6 meal prep containers (glass preferred for reheating).
2. **Lunch containers (5):** ~200g chicken tikka + 1 cup cooked rice + 1 cup cooked dal per container. Label by day.
3. **Dinner containers (2):** 200g goat keema in one, extra chicken in another.
4. **Side containers:** Palak sabzi in one container, hard-boiled eggs in another.
5. Store all in refrigerator. Chicken and dal are good for 4-5 days refrigerated.

| Clock | Task | Status |
|-------|------|--------|
| 0:00 | Start dal simmering + marinate chicken | Dal on stove, chicken in fridge |
| 0:10 | Start rice + start boiling eggs | Rice cooker on, eggs on stove |
| 0:15 | Make tadka for dal, finish dal | Dal done, set aside |
| 0:30 | Eggs done, ice bath. Preheat oven. | Eggs cooling |
| 0:35 | Chicken into oven (batch 1) | Chicken baking |
| 0:40 | Start goat keema on stovetop | Keema cooking |
| 0:55 | Flip/check chicken. Rice done, fluff. | Multi-tasking |
| 1:00 | Chicken batch 1 done. Batch 2 in oven if needed. | Keema nearly done |

| 1:20 | Everything cooked. Begin cooling. | Cooling |
| 1:30 | Peel eggs, portion everything into containers | Portioning |
| 1:45 | Clean up kitchen | Done |

**Total active time: ~55-65 minutes.** Total wall clock: ~1 hour 45 minutes to 2 hours.
